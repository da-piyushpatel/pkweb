Physics Books IIT-JEE

Confused which book to begin with for JEE? Let me help you.

I.E. Irodov Concept wise it's is not one, to begin with. Not only does it contain questions for higher brainstorming, but also it lacks absolutely any efforts to explain concepts to students. No good illustrations makes it even harder to visualise and begin solving with the question. Basically, it is a question bank and not to fit to be your first book first. But once you have build a strong foundation in physics concepts this book will definitly help you to ace your preprations. Click here to buy I.E. Irodov

H.C.Verma Truth be told, Concepts of Physics by H C Verma stays true to its name. It is single-handedly the best available book for concept building. The concise theory, solved examples makes it easy to brush up your concepts quickly and the MCQs are one of the best concept building tools I found in this book. Every chapter contains enough number of question to build your concepts brick by brick, each and every question is unique you won't find the repetetion in quesitons, which means you won't end up wasting your lot of time solving similar questions unlike other books.

Click here to buy H.C.Verma Part 1 | Click here to buy H.C.Verma Part 2 | Click here to buy H.C.Verma Combo



D.C. Pandey This book no dobut is a good one for beginners who want to extra help in JEE Physics. Personally I won't recommend this book to anyone who can solve HC Verma questions. You can consider this book as a bridge between JEE and Board level Physics. This book has six parts, and once you begin solving from this book you'll become addicted to it, the solved examples are very detailed and will help students with very weak foundation in Physics. For JEE Mains I would say this is the best book you can buy.

Choose a book from which you are able to solve atleast 5 questions in 10 questions, else either you will end up wasting a lot of time or feeling over-confident about your preprations.

S.S. Kortov This book is like I.E. Irodov so choose any one of these. There are several questions which are beyond the syllabus and if not solved judicially you may end up wasting a lot of you time.

Past Year Papers IIT-JEE Solving Past Year Papers cannot be ignored at all, so you need to have this book.
