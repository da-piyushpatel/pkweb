Chemistry Books IIT-JEE

Confused which book to begin with for JEE? Let me help you.

Physical Chemistry

R.C. Mukherjee Just the best concept developing book. It acts like the "Concepts of Physical Chemistry". The easiest way of approaching varied questions through mole concept is efficiently explained . Initially each chapter starts with a brief theory and general points then followed by numerous illustrative examples to show application in problems . Lastly a short exercise is given to practise concept application with answers given at right margin of each question with hints when necessary . Every chapter of JEE has been included and chapters of miscellaneous problems has been given at the end of book. This book has been wholly committed to concept development for physical chemistry .

Alternatively you can try Balaji Physical Chemistry. The book is a nicely written for IIT JEE(class 11 and 12). The book contains all types of questions Single correct MCQ, Multiple answer correct MCQs, assertion-reason, match the following etc. The level of the questions keep increasing, level-1, level-2 and level-3. The best part it contains Hints and solutions for few questions.



Inorganic Chemistry

NCERT remains the best book if you want to ace Inorganic Chemistry for JEE Mains and Advanced. The only problem with NCERT is that the content is too concise. It often happens that you may miss to read a line and a question might be asked from that line itself. In order to avoid such mishappening you can do one thing and that is to buy NCERT at Fingertips. I have already explained the benifits of these book in this article so go and read out if you havent already.

Organic Chemistry

Solomons, Fryhle & Snyder Organic Chemistry By far the best book in terms of theory. Detailed explanation of concepts but it's too thick to read entirely so use it judiciously. Good amount of theory and questions offer decent coverage on each topic.

Conceptual Problems by Pearson This book covers all the essential concepts of organic chemistry through 2750 multiple-choice questions (MCQs). Treat this book like a Question bank, it has got no theory. So keep this as your reference book.

Arihant Organic Chemstry The good point of this book is that it has got clear explanation along with good amount of questions and theory. Good for JEE Mains level as well.

Past Year papers and DPP

Past Year Papers IIT-JEE Solving Past Year Papers cannot be ignored at all, so you need to have this book.

NTA JEE Main Chapter-wise DPP Sheets Based on new pattern of JEE mains. Quality Question so that you won't end up wasting a lot of your time. Good for JEE mock tests.
